My Design Process

How I turn problems
into beautiful solutions.

Every great product starts with the right questions. Here's the repeatable framework I bring to every project — from pharmacy software to pet care platforms.

01
🔍
Phase One
Discover
Listen before you leap

Before a single pixel is drawn, I invest deeply in understanding the people I'm designing for. I talk to real users, observe their behaviour, align with stakeholders, and map the problem space so the team is solving the right problem — not just any problem.

Key Activities
  • Stakeholder interviews & kickoff workshops
  • User interviews (20+ sessions across projects)
  • Contextual observation & field research
  • Competitive analysis & heuristic evaluation
  • Defining research goals & success metrics
Deliverables
  • Research plan & discussion guide
  • Interview synthesis & affinity maps
  • Empathy maps
  • Competitive landscape report
🎙️ User Interviews 📋 Notion 🗂️ Miro 📊 Hotjar 🔎 Google Analytics
02
🎯
Phase Two
Define
Frame the right problem

Research data is only useful when it's shaped into clarity. I synthesise findings into personas, journey maps, and crisp problem statements that give the entire team — design, product, and engineering — a shared north star to build toward.

Key Activities
  • Synthesising research into themes & insights
  • Building user personas grounded in data
  • Mapping current-state & future-state journeys
  • Crafting problem statements (How Might We…)
  • Prioritising pain points by impact & frequency
Deliverables
  • User personas (2–4 archetypes)
  • Experience & journey maps
  • Problem statements
  • Jobs-to-be-done framework
👤 Personas 🗺️ Journey Mapping 🗂️ Miro 📋 FigJam 📌 Affinity Diagrams
03
💡
Phase Three
Ideate
Diverge wildly, converge sharply

With the problem clearly defined, I open up the solution space. Through sketching, crazy-8s, and design studio workshops I generate a wide range of concepts — then systematically narrow down to the most feasible, viable, and desirable ideas using structured prioritisation.

Key Activities
  • Crazy-8s & rapid sketching sessions
  • Design studio workshops with cross-functional teams
  • Concept development & scenario modelling
  • Feature prioritisation (MoSCoW / effort-impact matrix)
  • Information architecture & user flow mapping
Deliverables
  • Concept sketches & storyboards
  • User flow diagrams
  • Site map / IA document
  • Prioritised feature list
✏️ Sketching 🗂️ FigJam 🧩 Crazy-8s 📐 Flowcharts 📊 Priority Matrix
04
⚡
Phase Four
Prototype
Make ideas tangible, fast

I move from low-fidelity wireframes to interactive high-fidelity prototypes — but always at the right fidelity for the question being tested. I build on top of scalable design systems, ensuring every component is accessible, consistent, and developer-ready from day one.

Key Activities
  • Low-fi wireframes to validate layout & structure
  • High-fi mockups with production-ready UI
  • Interactive Figma prototypes for user testing
  • Design system & component library build-out
  • WCAG 2.2 accessibility review at every stage
Deliverables
  • Wireframes (lo-fi & hi-fi)
  • Interactive prototype
  • Design system / component library
  • Accessibility annotations
🎨 Figma 📱 Adobe XD ⚙️ Auto Layout ♿ WCAG Checker 🔗 Variants & Components
05
🔁
Phase Five
Test & Iterate
Real users, real answers

Design doesn't end at handoff — it ends when the product works for real people. I run structured usability tests, analyse results, and iterate rapidly. I track measurable outcomes post-launch and use data to continuously improve. Every decision I make is ultimately validated by the person using it.

Key Activities
  • Moderated & unmoderated usability testing
  • A/B testing to validate design decisions
  • Synthesising test findings into actionable insights
  • Developer handoff with specs & documentation
  • Post-launch tracking & iteration planning
Deliverables
  • Usability test report
  • Prioritised iteration backlog
  • Redline specs & handoff documentation
  • Post-launch metrics report
🧪 Maze 📹 Lookback 📊 Hotjar 🔬 A/B Testing 📝 Zeplin / Dev Mode
